Experiment 2: Autocorrelation and Power Spectral Density of AWGN

Instructions
  • This experiment demonstrates two key statistical properties of an AWGN signal.
  • Use the **Noise Power** slider to set the variance (\(\sigma^2\)) of the Gaussian noise signal. Press **Generate New Signal** to create a new realization.
  • The **Autocorrelation Plot** shows how correlated the signal is with a time-shifted version of itself. For white noise, this should be a sharp impulse at a time lag of 0, and nearly zero everywhere else.
  • The **Power Spectral Density (PSD)** plot is the Fourier Transform of the autocorrelation. For white noise, the power is distributed evenly across all frequencies, resulting in a flat spectrum.
  • Use the **Frequency Band** sliders to select a range of interest and see the calculated noise power within that band in the Observations panel.
