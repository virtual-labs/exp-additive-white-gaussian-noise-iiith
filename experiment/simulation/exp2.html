Visualizing Noise Correlation with Gaussian Contours

Instructions

This experiment reveals the fundamental statistical difference between white and colored noise by visualizing their joint probability distributions.

Key Concepts

  • White Noise: Any two samples are statistically independent. Plotting the noise at time `t` vs. `t-τ` results in a circularly symmetric probability distribution.
  • Colored Noise: Samples are correlated. This correlation skews the joint distribution into a tilted ellipse.

How to Interact

  • The main chart shows a live scatter plot of noise samples `X(t)` vs. `X(t-τ)`, with the theoretical Gaussian contours overlaid.
  • The side chart shows the noise waveform in the time domain for context.
  • Use the "Noise 'Color' (Filter Strength)" slider to apply a low-pass filter to the white noise.
  • Observe how the contours change from perfect circles (uncorrelated) for white noise to tilted ellipses (correlated) as you increase the filter strength.
