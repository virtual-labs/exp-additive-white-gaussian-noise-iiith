Identifying ACF and PSD of Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN)

Instructions

This experiment tests your understanding of the fundamental properties of Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN), a key concept in communication systems and signal processing.

Objective

  • To correctly identify the Autocorrelation Function (ACF) and Power Spectral Density (PSD) of ideal white noise from a set of options.

Key Concepts

  • Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN):
    • White: The "white" property implies that the noise has constant power across all frequencies. Its Power Spectral Density (PSD) is flat.
    • Uncorrelated: A direct consequence of the "white" property is that the noise values at any two different points in time are completely uncorrelated. Its Autocorrelation Function (ACF) is an impulse.
  • Autocorrelation Function (ACF): Measures the correlation of a signal with a delayed version of itself. For white noise, this should be non-zero only at a zero time lag (Ï„=0).
  • Power Spectral Density (PSD): Describes how the power of a signal is distributed over frequency. For white noise, this should be constant for all frequencies.
